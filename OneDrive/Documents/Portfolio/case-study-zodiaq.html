01 / Overview

What is ZODIAQ?

ZODIAQ is a responsive astrology website where users can explore their daily horoscope, birth chart (Kundali), love compatibility, zodiac traits, and book live consultations with verified astrologers — all in one place.

Most astrology websites feel outdated or cluttered. ZODIAQ was designed to be calm, modern, and beginner-friendly — something that works well on both desktop and mobile. It’s not just about predictions, it’s about creating a personal connection with the stars.

02 / My Work

What I did on this project.

  • Studied what other astrology apps are doing (good and bad)
  • Designed wireframes for all key flows
  • Created all final UI screens in Figma
  • Collected user feedback and made improvements
  • Designed both the user side and the astrologer dashboard
03 / Problem

What’s wrong with existing astrology apps?

Many astrology websites today are either too complicated, visually outdated, or filled with too much information that confuses new users.

  • Users struggle to find trusted astrologers and can’t tell who’s genuine
  • Booking a consultation is confusing — unclear pricing, too many steps
  • Understanding a birth chart is hard due to poor layouts
  • Astrologers don’t have a proper space to manage clients, generate reports, or track bookings
04 / Goals

What I wanted to achieve.

  • Make astrology simple and accessible — especially for beginners
  • Build trust through clean design and verified astrologer profiles
  • Let users easily book consultations, generate Kundalis, and read horoscopes
  • Give astrologers useful tools — Kundali maker, CRM, Panchang finder, Vastu analysis
  • Design a fully responsive website for both mobile and desktop
  • Create smooth onboarding for both users and astrologers
05 / Research

What I learned from competitors.

I studied three major platforms to understand what they do well and where they fall short:

Feature AstroSage AstroSure AstroTalk
UI Design Outdated & crowded Clean & modern Basic, not engaging
User Experience Confusing navigation Smooth & friendly Average, lacks personal touch
Live Consultation Available but clunky Not available Easy booking + live chat
Reports Many but hard to find Fewer options Well-packaged & detailed
Mobile Cluttered Good Accessible

Key pain points I found:

  • Users find most interfaces visually overwhelming and hard to navigate
  • It’s hard to find relevant information quickly on content-heavy platforms
  • Consultation booking flows are complicated or unreliable
06 / Process

How I approached the design.

I followed a step-by-step approach, keeping the user at the center of every decision:

  • Understanding the domain — Learned about astrology terminology, user expectations, and what makes people trust an astrologer
  • User research & competitor analysis — Studied AstroSage, AstroTalk, AstroSure to find gaps
  • Client meetings & brainstorming — Aligned on features, priorities, and user flows
  • Wireframing — Sketched out all key screens before jumping to final UI
  • Final UI design — High-fidelity screens in Figma with consistent design system
  • Feedback & revisions — Collected feedback and refined the designs
07 / Design Decisions

Why things look the way they do.

Every design choice was made to keep things clean and build trust:

Element What I Did Why
Smart Filters Filter by concern (Career, Love, Health) Helps users find the right astrologer fast without scrolling endlessly
Booking UI One-tap video/audio call People looking for guidance don’t want to fill 5 forms first
Color Palette Warm earth tones (brown/amber) Feels calm and spiritual — not like a flashy gaming app
Verification Badges Star rating system for astrologers Users can immediately see who’s verified and trusted

09 / Challenges

What was hard about this project.

One of the biggest challenges was finding the right balance between looking beautiful and being easy to use. Astrology platforms often use rich, decorative visuals to create a mystical vibe — but too many design elements started hurting readability and making the booking flow harder to follow.

I had to strip back some of my initial ideas and focus on what actually helped the user complete their task.

10 / What I Learned

This project taught me a lot.

Designing ZODIAQ was one of my most meaningful projects. It challenged me to design for a niche audience, simplify complex information (like Kundali charts and Panchang data), and create an experience that feels trustworthy.

From the first brainstorming session to collecting final feedback — every step of the process taught me how to balance visual design with real usability. I’m proud of how the final product turned out.
